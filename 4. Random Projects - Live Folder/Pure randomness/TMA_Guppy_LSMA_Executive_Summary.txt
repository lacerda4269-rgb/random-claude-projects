EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
TMA Overlay / Guppy GMMA / LSMA 21 — Cross Signals Guide (v3)
========================================================================

WHAT THIS DOCUMENT IS

A guide to three chart indicators — tools that draw lines over a price
chart to help read what the market is doing — and, more importantly,
how the three work together as one system. Each tool runs at a
different speed and answers a different question. None of them tells
you WHERE to enter a trade; that job belongs to price structure
(support and resistance levels). These three decide whether a trade
at that spot is worth taking.


THE THREE TOOLS, IN PLAIN ENGLISH

1. TMA RIBBON (the slow one) — "What kind of market is this?"

   Four smoothed average lines of price (21, 50, 100, and 200 bars),
   read as two groups: a short group (average of the 21 and 50) and a
   long group (average of the 100 and 200). The gap between the two
   groups — and whether both are pointing the same way — tells you the
   market's big-picture condition (the "regime"): broadly rising or
   broadly falling.

   Why groups instead of single lines? Any single line gives false
   hope — the fast line bends upward on every little bounce even in a
   falling market. The group construction filters that out. A real
   turn requires BOTH groups moving the same direction.

   Key point: this tool is deliberately slow. It confirms big changes
   late, but it is rarely wrong. It is never traded off directly — it
   just sets the backdrop.

2. GUPPY GMMA (the middle one) — "Who is in this market?"

   Two bundles of six lines each. The fast bundle (the "trader
   ribbon") tracks short-term traders; the slow bundle (the "investor
   ribbon") tracks longer-term money. What matters is how wide each
   bundle is fanned out (wide = agreement/conviction, squeezed
   together = indecision), and whether the fast bundle is above or
   below the slow one.

   The earliest useful signal: when both bundles squeeze tight (the
   market "coiling") and then the trader ribbon fans out in one
   direction — that fires before any official crossover.

   The hardest rule in the whole document lives here: when the trader
   ribbon is in EXTREME expansion (wide, fanned, accelerating), you do
   not bet against it. Extreme bullish = no shorts. Extreme bearish =
   no longs. No exceptions. This is called a kill gate.

3. LSMA 21 (the fast one) — "Does this exact moment confirm?"

   A statistical best-fit trend line through the last 21 price bars —
   not an average of the past, but the current trend projected to
   right now. You read two things: its slope (which way it points and
   how steeply) and its curvature (whether it is speeding up or
   slowing down).

   Because it is fast, it gives false signals in choppy markets. Two
   rules tame it: (1) a "dead zone" — a nearly flat slope counts as no
   signal at all; (2) curvature only counts when it agrees with slope.
   A falling line that is "curving up" is a decline slowing down, NOT
   a market turning up.

   It is used only at decision points — when price reaches a level
   that matters, this tool says "momentum confirms" or "momentum
   rejects."


HOW THEY WORK TOGETHER

Three speeds, three questions:
   - TMA (slowest):  What is the market doing overall?
   - Guppy (middle): Who is participating, and is the move backed?
   - LSMA (fastest): Does momentum confirm at this exact moment?

The playbook the document lays out:

   FULL ALIGNMENT — all three point the same way: highest-conviction
   condition. Trade only in that direction; ignore counter-signals.

   FAST LAYER DISAGREES — the slow tools say uptrend, the LSMA flips
   down: that is a pullback, not a reversal. You wait. The
   highest-quality recurring setup in this system is the fast layer
   rotating BACK into agreement right at a structural level.

   EARLY REVERSAL — the two faster tools turn against a still-intact
   slow ribbon: earliest tradeable evidence of a real change. Smaller
   size until the Guppy fully confirms; the TMA is always last to
   admit it.

   NO-TRADE — all three tangled, flat, or in dead zones: no regime
   exists. Standing down IS the correct output.


THE RULES WORTH REMEMBERING

   - Never fight a fully ordered, separating ribbon. Lowest-
     probability trade available.
   - Extreme Guppy expansion is an absolute kill gate — no counter-
     trades, period.
   - Slowing down is not turning around. A narrowing spread or an
     upward curve during a decline is deceleration, not reversal.
   - The entry spot always comes from price structure. The three
     indicators only qualify, filter, and confirm it.
   - Disagreement across all three speeds means stand down.


BOTTOM LINE (the document's own rule of thumb)

The TMA ribbon tells you what the market is. The Guppy tells you who
is in it. The LSMA tells you whether the moment confirms. Structure
picks the spot — these three decide whether the spot gets traded.
