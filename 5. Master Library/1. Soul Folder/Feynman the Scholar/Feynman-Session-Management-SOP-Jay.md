## What This SOP Covers

This document explains how your Feynman sessions are recorded and organized. Short version: each [unit] keeps its own session log in the [unit]'s support docs folder, and there's one project-wide lessons log that Feynman updates when he learns something worth carrying into future [units].

---

## The Three Files You'll Never Need to Touch

**1. [Unit] Session Summary** — lives inside each [unit]'s support docs folder.
Example: `[Unit] - Support Doc Folder - PM Lead\[course-code]-Session-Summary\[course-code]-Session-Summary.md`
Feynman writes to this at the end of every session. It's a running record of what was worked, how the gate check went, and where things stand.

**2. [Program] Lessons Log** — lives in `[Feynman P/M Lead Folder]\[Program] Lessons Log.md`
This one is different. It doesn't get an entry every session. Feynman only adds something here when a lesson from your current [unit] will genuinely change how he approaches your next [unit]. Think of it as the "what we learned doing this [program]" file — built over time, not filled in automatically.

**3. Master Outcomes Log** — lives in `[Feynman P/M Lead Folder]\Master-Outcomes-Log.md`
One entry per completed [unit], added at [unit] close-out. Feynman writes it; you don't need to do anything. It's your [program] record in plain language — what you took, what you completed, what you came away knowing. It grows with every finished [unit] until your [program] is done.

---

## What You Do at the Start of a Feynman Session

One thing: tell Feynman which [unit] you're working on (or it'll be obvious from context).

Feynman will run the gate check: **"Any [unit] updates this week?"**
- **No** → Feynman does a quick color recalculation on all deliverables and updates the Structure Map - Visual Tracker if anything crossed a threshold (the calendar moved, so some items may have shifted from White to Yellow, or Yellow to Orange, even without any changes). Nothing else changes.
- **Yes** → Feynman updates your Weekly Trackers based on what you tell him, then recalculates.

Either way, the color check always runs. Time passing alone can push a deliverable from White to Yellow even if nothing else changed.

---

## Telling the Team When You Submit Something

When you submit an assignment, let Feynman, Sophia, or Sage know — just say "I submitted [item]." Feynman will flip that item to Green on the Structure Map - Visual Tracker. Green means submitted, not just "plenty of time."

You don't have to do this immediately — but the sooner you tell someone, the sooner the map reflects reality. If you forget, Feynman may ask you as a reminder, especially when he's doing a color pass.

**Before anything goes Black (past due):** Feynman will always check with you first — *"Did you submit [item]? Should this be Black or Green?"* He won't mark anything past due without asking you first. This way, nothing gets marked wrong just because the date passed.

---

## What Happens at the End of a Feynman Session

Feynman writes the session record. Sophia files it and pushes to GitHub. You don't need to do anything.

If it was a mixed session (you worked on IP stuff AND a [unit]), Sophia closes both summaries in the same push.

Once in a while a [unit] session runs in its own separate window at the same time as a main session. When that happens, Feynman wraps up his [unit] record in that window and gives Sage a heads-up (through you). Sophia then folds Feynman's closed entries into the main close and sends everything up in one push. Sage looks it over before it's committed. You don't need to do anything different.

---

## When a New [Unit] Starts (COURSESTART)

Feynman and Sophia set up the [unit] session files at COURSESTART — not mid-session. By the time you say "go," the files are ready.

---

## When a [Unit] Ends

Feynman does a close-out pass: reviews the full session summary, promotes anything worth keeping to the [Program] Lessons Log, and marks the [unit] complete on the Structure Map - Visual Tracker. Old [unit] files stay exactly where they are — they don't get moved or deleted.

---

## Key Rules

- You work one [unit] at a time — always. No two active [units] in the same session.
- The [unit] session summary and index are always updated together — never one without the other.
- The [Program] Lessons Log is selective — most sessions produce no entry, and that's correct.
